# Project Summary

## Context

This project, associated with Texas A&M University-Corpus Christi, evaluated a capital budgeting decision for Rio Negro Inc. The company needed to decide whether to overhaul an existing cargo ship or purchase a new replacement vessel.

## Portfolio Framing

The public version highlights the finance analytics value:

- Converting a business decision into comparable financial alternatives
- Modeling after-tax cash flows across different time horizons
- Applying NPV to same-horizon repair options
- Applying equivalent annual cost to compare alternatives with different useful lives
- Translating quantitative outputs into a clear executive recommendation

## Decision Alternatives

The case compared three alternatives:

- Basic overhaul of the existing vessel
- Advanced overhaul with a new engine and control system
- Purchase of a new cargo ship

## Interpretation

The advanced overhaul was more cost-effective than the basic overhaul among the two 12-year repair alternatives. However, the new vessel had the lowest equivalent annual cost over the long-term comparison period and provided additional operational advantages, including automated navigation, improved accommodations, and larger storage capacity.

The recommendation was to purchase the new vessel.
